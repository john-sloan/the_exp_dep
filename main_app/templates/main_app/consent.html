Information Sheet

Title:    An investigation into the response of native and non-native speakers to change in facial expression of a 3D avatar to their typed sentences.

Topic:    Second Language Acquisition, Computational Linguistics, Multimodal Communication

Institution:    UCD Computer Science

Researcher:    John Sloan

 

What is this research about?

I am studying language learning and emotions. I want to understand the difference between how language learners and native speakers feel when they see different facial expressions in a 3D avatar.

Why are you doing this research?

I believe facial expressions are a very important means of non-verbal communication. I want to understand what language learners think when they see different expressions and use this to help people acquire languages.

Why have I been invited to take part?

This study is open to all native English speakers and non-native English learners of intermediate ability and up.

What will happen if I decide to take part in this research study?

You will take a 15-minute quiz which has 2 sections: first, you will answer some questionson emotion & language; and second, you will type 6 sentences while you can see the facial expressions of an avatar.

How will the data be used?

I will use your answers from the questions to understand your English ability and what you think of the avatarâ€™s expressions. I will use the sentences you typed on computer to understand how you change your language production after seeing various expressions.

How will you protect my privacy?

This study is anonymous. You should not enter your real name or the names of others while taking part.

How is this research funded?

This project is partly funded through the ADAPT centre. ADAPT reviews papers in advance of submission to conferences/journals in case there are any opportunities to protect/commercialise the research.

What are the benefits of taking part in this research study?

You will be able to practice your English. You will interact with an avatar who responds to your sentences.

What are the risks of taking part in this research study?

If you see negative expressions from the avatar you may feel bad.

Can I change my mind at any stage and withdraw from the study?

Yes. You can change your mind anytime and withdraw with no disadvantage.

Has this research project received ethical approval?

Yes. Approval from the UCD Human Research Ethics comittee was granted on 25/01/2017.

How will I find out what happens with this project?

You can email - john.sloan.1@ucdconnect.ie or phone - 00447470180188.
